Can Macroeconomic Indicators Predict Unemployment Rates?
Author: Apoorva Somani | Course: ECO 6810 | Final submission

What this project does
Trains a Random Forest model on nine World Bank macro and structural indicators
to predict country-level unemployment rates. The model is evaluated on a
randomly held-out 20% test set (primary metric) and on a stricter temporal
holdout — trained on 2000–2018, tested on 2019–2023 — to demonstrate
out-of-time generalisability.
Primary result: out-of-sample R² = 0.6841 (threshold 0.45, passed).
Temporal validation: R² = 0.3612 on 2019–2023 (includes 2020 COVID shock).
This is a predictive project. The model flags countries where unemployment
is anomalously high or low relative to macro fundamentals. It does not
establish causal effects.

The GDP growth hypothesis was not supported. This is reported as a substantive
finding: unemployment in a cross-country panel is driven by structural features
(labour market participation, urbanisation, demographics), not short-run growth
cycles. Okun's Law operates mainly at the within-country time-series level.
